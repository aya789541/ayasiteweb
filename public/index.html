The Science Behind It

At its core, the science is straightforward: hydrogen combustion produces water vapor, unlike carbon-rich fuels that emit carbon dioxide. This fundamental difference means that blending hydrogen with natural gas intrinsically reduces the carbon content of the resultant feed.

However, while the carbon emission benefits are evident, it's crucial to address the potential for nitrogen oxides (NOx) formation during hydrogen combustion at elevated temperatures. Such challenges, while notable, can be managed with advanced combustion techniques and continuous monitoring.
