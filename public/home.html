References

The program outlined here is designed to estimate greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions and assess energy efficiency within APO's operational sphere. It's based on standards set by the American Petroleum Institute (API) and the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA), enhanced with CR360 environmental management solutions. The following references have been pivotal in shaping our program:

  • American Petroleum Institute (API): Provides technical data and industry-specific insights for GHG emissions estimation. [ Read More ]
  • U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA): Offers guidelines for quantifying and reporting GHG emissions in line with regulatory mandates. [ Read More ]
  • Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC): Provides internationally recognized standards for GHG emissions quantification. [ Read More ]
  • CR360: The CR360 environmental management solutions have been instrumental in enabling proficient management of GHG emissions, thereby augmenting our program’s efficacy in aligning with contemporary emissions reduction paradigms. Accessible at CR360 Environmental Management Solutions .

These esteemed references collectively undergird the rigorous methodological approach adopted in our program, ensuring that the estimations and appraisals conducted for APO are both accurate and congruent with prevailing industry standards and regulatory stipulations, while also embracing modern emissions management solutions.
