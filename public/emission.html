GHG Emissions Insights

The APOCore Emission Framework, developed specifically for APO, facilitates the intricate process of quantifying greenhouse gas emissions from stationary combustion, categorized under Scope 1 emissions in the natural gas sector. Emphasizing precision and diligence, the framework ensures that each piece of equipment associated with stationary combustion undergoes a meticulous assessment to determine its emissions profile.

Emissions estimation is segmented by equipment type, with special attention to those used in stationary combustion processes. This differentiation acknowledges the varied emission potentials and dynamics across equipment sets specific to this scope. By adopting such a granular approach, the aim is to compile an emissions inventory that is comprehensive and accurately reflective of stationary combustion operations.

Through the rigorous implementation of this framework, there's a strong commitment to environmental stewardship. This aligns APO's stationary combustion operations with industry best practices and contributes significantly to global efforts in mitigating greenhouse gas emissions from such processes.

Turbines Emission Estimation

Emissions from turbines are assessed using a dual methodology. Emission factors sourced from the API and the AP-42 Chapter 3, Section 1: Stationary Gas Turbines, provide foundational data. An energy balance approach complements these factors.

Source: API GHG Compendium & EPA AP-42 Chapter 3, Section 1

Heaters Emission Estimation

For heaters, emission evaluation uses factors from the API and the AP-42 Chapter 4, Section 1: Natural Gas Combustion. An energy balance assessment enhances the accuracy of these estimations.

Source: API GHG Compendium & EPA AP-42 Chapter 4, Section 1

Incinerators Emission Estimation

The approach for incinerators mirrors that of heaters, utilizing emission factors from the API and the AP-42 Chapter 4, Section 1: Natural Gas Combustion, complemented by an energy balance assessment.

Source: API GHG Compendium & EPA AP-42 Chapter 4, Section 1

Flares Emission Estimation

Emissions from flares are determined using factors from the AP-42 Chapter 13, Section 1: Industrial Flares, combined with data from the API. An energy balance methodology is also applied.

Source: API GHG Compendium & EPA AP-42 Chapter 13, Section 1
