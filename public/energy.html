Efficiency Guide

Within the framework established at APO, a particular emphasis is placed on assessing the energy and thermal efficiency of critical components, notably turbines and heaters. This scrutiny is paramount, given the significant role these units play in the overall operational efficiency of the natural gas sector.

For turbines, the calculations offer a detailed insight into energy conversion rates, presenting a clear picture of their operational efficiency. Similarly, when assessing heaters, the emphasis is on deriving their thermal efficiency, representing the effectiveness of heat transfer processes.

Through the rigorous implementation of this framework, there's a strong commitment to environmental stewardship. This aligns APO's stationary combustion operations with industry best practices and contributes significantly to global efforts in mitigating greenhouse gas emissions from such processes.

Efficiency Assessment for Fired Process Heaters

This method evaluates the efficiency of gaseous fuel-based fired process heaters. Factors considered include ambient conditions like temperature and humidity, estimated radiation losses, exit temperatures of flue gas, oxygen content in flue gas, composition of fuel gas, and fuel supply temperatures. Both 'wet basis' and 'dry basis' oxygen sampling are accommodated. The results provide details on flue gas composition relative to the mass of fuel gas burned, net and gross thermal efficiencies, as well as overall fuel efficiency.

Gas Turbine Power Estimations

The process aims to determine the site-specific power output of a gas turbine under given operational conditions. While base references can offer foundational insights, the actual site power is notably affected by several factors. Crucial determinants include ambient temperature, humidity levels, and inherent losses experienced during both the inlet and exhaust phases. Additionally, while natural gas consistently serves as the fuel source, variations in its molar composition can significantly influence the turbine's output. By meticulously accounting for these elements, this methodology furnishes a thorough understanding of the turbine's site-specific performance, facilitating more astute operational and maintenance strategies.
